---
title: "the biggest problem with software engineering"
date: 2026-04-30
tags: ["software", "engineering"]
draft: false
---

the profit motive. that's it.

i was yet again met with incredulous eyes yesterday, after telling another software engineer that i spend my time doing projects just to scratch my technical itch - to try new things and see if i can build them, making them available on github and hoping that someone else will benefit from them. honestly, it would be an _honor_ if anyone ever uses anything i made, and i wouldn't mind developing the projects further should any user give me feedback (sparingly, of course. most feedbacks are bad).

this is the state of software engineering now, at least where i'm from. if there's no money to be made, it's not worth the time. forget about passion, or creativity, or `<insert any good human quality here>`. it's all gone. everyone wants a quick, easy way to make money in this industry. money, money, money. the more you say it, the more convinced you are of its absolutes.

it wasn't always this way. richard stallman didn't create the most-used utility library right now thinking of monetizing it. so was python's guido von rossum, or linux's linus torvald, or ffmpeg's fabrice bellard. and their products don't just work, they run the world for decades, and for decades to come. if they had $1 for every user they'd be insanely rich (not to mention if they charge subscription like companies nowadays). it seems the world stopped making those exceptional people. and in the case of my country, they probably never started.
